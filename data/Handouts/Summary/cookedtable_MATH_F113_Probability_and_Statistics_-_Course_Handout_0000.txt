
Course: MATH F113 Probability and Statistics
Summary:
The course covers fundamental concepts in Probability and Statistics. Topics include a quick review of probability concepts, sample spaces, events, axioms, interpretations, conditional probability, and independence (Lec. No. 1-3). The subsequent lectures (Lec. No. 4-5) focus on defining random variables, exploring discrete distributions, and understanding expected values and moment generating functions. The course progresses to cover specific distributions such as the Binomial Probability Distribution, Hypergeometric, and Negative Binomial (Lec. No. 6-9). Relevant chapters from the textbook are indicated for each topic.
